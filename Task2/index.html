Sardar Vallabhbhai Javerbhai Patel

(1875-1950)

About Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel

Sardar Vallabhbhai Javerbhai Patel (1875-1950), the Iron Man of our Nation, is popularly known as Sardar Patel. He gave his whole, serving our nation as a First Deputy Prime Minister as as first Home Minister. His dream has always been to integrate the whole country and bring unity between the people. The commitment he made to bring integration between all the people, was just amazing. Due to his consistent efforts and contributions, he is being honoured as 'The Iron Man of India'. 'Statue of Unity', the world's tallest statue, which is approx. 597ft (182m), established in 2018 in Gujarat, in memory of him.

Key points about Sardar Patel:

1.Unity and Integration: Sardar Patel is often referred to as the "Iron Man of India" for his pivotal role in the integration of princely states into a unified India after independence. He worked tirelessly to persuade the princely states to accede to India, ensuring the integrity and unity of the nation.

2. Deputy Prime Minister and Home Minister: After India gained independence in 1947, Sardar Patel became the country's first Deputy Prime Minister and the first Minister of Home Affairs. In these roles, he played a crucial part in establishing and strengthening the administrative and political foundations of India.

3.Administrative Reforms: Sardar Patel was instrumental in initiating administrative reforms in India. He worked towards the establishment of an efficient civil service and played a key role in drafting the Indian Administrative Service (IAS) and the Indian Police Service (IPS).

4.Bardoli Satyagraha: Sardar Patel emerged as a leader during the Bardoli Satyagraha in 1928. The movement, led by Patel, aimed to protest against excessive taxes imposed by the British on farmers in the Bardoli region of Gujarat. The successful outcome of the movement established Patel as a prominent figure in the national struggle for independence.
